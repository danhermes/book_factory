# Introduction

*ChatGPT for Business* is a field guide to transformation.

Not the abstract kind that lives in white papers and keynote slides, but the practical, roll-up-your-sleeves kind that happens when AI starts to touch the core of your company. This book is about how your organization can evolve by embedding intelligence—true, adaptive, conversational intelligence—into its deepest functions.

This isn’t a guide to prompting ChatGPT.  Those books exist and one of them, *Prompt Power*, I wrote myself. That book taught teaches you how to wield ChatGPT with power and precision. This book is about you and everyone you work with: your teams, your groups, your customers, partners, and your organization. But we're going to use ChatGPT to help us get there, as a way to build bridges between how you work now and how your business could operate in a more intelligent, responsive, and resilient future. The ChatGPT prompts in these pages aren’t just tools, they’re highway ramps. 

[2/3 tools, 1/3 ramps]

The change we’re after isn’t just automation. It’s augmentation. Not just productivity gains, but capability leaps. This book calls that leap *AI Transformation*. And we’ll explore how it takes shape across six essential business domains:

- Customer Experience
- Process Automation
- Data-Driven Decision Making
- Innovation and Product Development
- Workforce Transformation
- Risk Management and Compliance

Each chapter examines one of these areas through a deceptively simple lens: What happens when you start asking better questions? Not to your team—but to your AI. 

To make the mental leap, the paradigm shift, you don’t need a developer team. You don’t need a data lake. You don’t need a master’s in machine learning. All you need is a question that matters to your business, and the willingness to have a conversation with the future.

Each chapter begins with a business scenario to show what AI transformation looks like from the inside. From there, you’ll find techniques, strategies, and prompts that help you apply these same shifts inside your own organization.

This book is not an implementation guide. It’s a book to connect the dots. Bring your own data, your own problems, and your own curiosity and you’ll walk away not just with new tools, but with a new mental model. One where your business works smarter, empathetically, cooperatively, and where strategy isn’t bound by bandwidth.

By the end of this journey, my hope is that you’ll see AI not as a cost, or a threat, or even as a feature—but as a collaborator. A trustworthy colleague, fluent in every function of your business, capable of working with you across every department, every challenge, and every opportunity.

This isn’t a book about what AI can do.

It’s a book about what you can do—with it.
