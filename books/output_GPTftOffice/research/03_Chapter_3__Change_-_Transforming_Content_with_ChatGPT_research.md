### Prompts That Go Awry

**Section Type**: Net Zero Prompt

In the process of transforming content, not all prompts yield desired results. Misguided prompts can lead to outputs that are off-brand or irrelevant. A classic example is requesting verbose detailed content where brevity is more impactful.

For example:

Faulty Prompt:
  
```
"Write a 2000-word article from our quarterly financial data, focusing on every minor detail."
```
Effective Prompt:

```
"Summarize our quarterly financial data into a 3-minute read targeting young professionals, highlighting key growth metrics and future projections."
```

Avoiding pitfalls involves crafting prompts that are clear, concise, and tailored to the end goal. Best practices involve understanding the audience and intended use while setting clear deliverables.
